What Do the Colors Represent?

In short:
This pronunciation practice system analyzes your voice and shows it as colors. The colors represent voice characteristics, and if your colors are similar to the reference voice, your pronunciation is likely similar.

Red-ish color
Pitch (high or low voice)
Green-ish color
Resonance / tone quality
Blue-ish color
Strength / loudness of voice
Example: Chinese "mǔ"
If the reference voice shows red, pink, or orange, try to make your voice produce similar colors. It doesn’t have to match exactly—similar shades are enough.
Red-ish color
"a-↑" (high pitch)
"a-↓" (low pitch)
Green-ish color
"i" (spread mouth, bright sound)
"u" (rounded mouth, muffled sound)
Blue-ish color
"e!" (loud)
"e..." (soft)
Supplement
"o-" (clear) vs "o..." (muffled)

Tips for Use

  • Remember the color of the reference voice (on the right)
  • Press the record button to record your own voice
  • Compare your colors with the reference voice
  • Practice repeatedly until your colors are similar
  • Use the score to track improvement

Helpful Info

The same person saying the same word may produce slightly different colors each time. Instead of aiming for an exact match, focus on "roughly similar shades."

Important Note

Even if colors are similar, it does not guarantee correct pronunciation. This system is a practice aid. If possible, get guidance from native speakers or teachers as well.

Summary:
Colors are like a "fingerprint" of your voice. Practice until your color fingerprint is similar to the reference, and your pronunciation will naturally improve. Enjoy practicing and progress step by step!
